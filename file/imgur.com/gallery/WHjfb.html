9 Under-rated films that are fun to watch!

Shoot 'Em Up

2007 Clive Owen, Paul Giamatti, Monica Bellucci When Clive Owen finds a baby, he is sent on a wild game of cat-and-mouse to get the baby to safey, despite Paul Giamatti's attempts to kill the baby. This over-the-top, John-Woo style movie has little plot, a whole lot of action, and is a great movie for when you want something fun, not too serious and sometimes just plain wacky!

The Spirit

2008 Gabriel Macht, Eva Medes, Sarah Paulson, Scarlett Johansson and Samuel L. Jackson as The Octopus When a Central City cop's life is ended, he wakes up with amazing powers, and vows to keep his city safe. But The Octopus has other plans for Central City. This film is straight off the comic's pages, with the same enhanced-color look that made Sin City a hit. A great choice for when you want to see an over-the-top comic villain take on a similarly over-the-top hero with hilarious results!

Stranger than Fiction

2007 Will Ferrell, Maggie Gyllenhaal, Dustin Hoffman, Queen Latifah and Emma Thompson When boring-as-dirt Harold Crick wakes up one morning to hear a mysterious voice narrating his life, he must discover who she is before his untimely, inevitable end arrives. Personally, I'm not a fan of Will Ferrell, but I love this film, even his performance. This is a quieter, enjoyable film about becoming more than you are when facing your own mortality.

The Terminal

2004 Tom Hanks, Catherine Zeta-Jones While flying to America from his home country, Viktor Navorski finds out that his country has been overthrown and he can not leave the airport. Tom Hanks delivers again on this quiet film about fulfilling a promise, and along the way, making friends, getting a job, finding romance and discovering America itself. A perfect film for a quiet night with the one you love, or for those feeling homesick and trapped.

Inside Man

2006 Denzel Washington, Clive Owen, Jodie Foster When a criminal mastermind (Clive) operates the perfect bank robbery, a determined detective (Denzel) must find a way to defuse the situation before it all goes bad. You've never quite seen a heist film like this before! Spike Lee directs this amazing work that shows that you can't always trust what you think!

Planet Terror

2007 Rose McGowan, Freddy Rodriguez, Michael Biehn, Jeff Fahey, Josh Prolin and Marley Shelton Anything I say about the "plot" of the film wouldn't do it justice. Created in the grand old style of the 1970's Grindhouse films, Planet Terror pits Doctors, nurses, marines and one very talented stripper against each other and a horde of Zombies in this amazing Zombie apocalypse film! For when when just want to laugh at the hilarity that makes up this film, this is for you!

Down Periscope

2003 Kelsey Grammer, Lauren Holly, Rob Schneider, Harry Dean Stanton, Bruce Dern and Rip Torn When the Navy's worst-performing skipper asks for his own nuclear submarine, he's given a 50-year-old diesel sub, a crew of misfits and the job of an enemy sub in a series of war-games. This hilarious comedy is sure to bring a smile to your face as Kelsey Grammer takes on the US Navy in order to get his dream boat.

The Island

2005 Ewan McGregor, Scarlett Johansson Living in a futuristic world where a deadly disease has ravaged the world, Ewan and Scarlett are among a small group of survivors, all wanting to make their way to The Island, the last habital place on Earth. But their world is shocked, when they discover that they aren't really the survivors, but clones of rich and famous people. Can they escape a near-certain death and find a way to live their own lives? This action-packed sci-fi adventure by Michael Bay is a fun journey through a time in our not-too-distant future.

Lord of War

2005 Nicolas Cage, Jared Leto and Ethan Hawke When Yuri Orlov discovers his talent for illegal arms dealing, he quickly reaches the top his trade, using his skill and quick wits, all the while being dogged by an incorruptible Interpol agent. This shocking film will let you see what arm dealing is really like, close-up.
