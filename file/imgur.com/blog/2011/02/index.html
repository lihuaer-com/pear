New Account Managment

Saturday, February 12th, 2011

The “Images” page has just been revamped to include many new features. It’s now possible to view images in specific albums, order images by date, name, or views, as well as generate links for many images at once. You can of course do everything you used to as well, such as delete and edit images.

If you have any feedback on it, then we’d love to hear from you!

Deleting Images

To delete your images, select “Delete Images” from the right-most dropdown.

You can now select any image you want, and as many as you want, for deletion. Once you’re done, just select the “Delete” button. You may also select the “Hide” button if you want to get rid of the images from your account without actually deleting them.

Viewing Album Images

You can now view images that are in a specific album, or even create an album before uploading. Just select an album from the first dropdown, and only the images from within that album will be displayed.

Sorting Images

To sort your images by time uploaded, original file name, or by the number of views, click on the second dropdown.

To change the order of the images, such as arranging them by the least number of views first, click on the third dropdown.

Viewing Image Info and Getting Links

To get information on an image such as the number of views it has, or to get its link codes, click on “View Image Info” from the right-most dropdown. This is also the default action when accessing the “Images” page. Now, once you click on a thumbnail, the image’s information will be displayed. You can copy links and share the images from here.

Generating Multiple Image Links

A new feature is the ability to generate multiple image links. Just select “Generate Image Links” from the right-most dropdown. You can now select any images you want and generate the link codes for them. Just click the “Done” button and the links will appear.

Editing Images

The editing works just like deleting and the generation of link codes. Just select “Edit Images” from the rightmost dropdown. From here, you’ll be able to select as many images as you want. When you’re done, just click “Edit” and an image-editing window will appear.

Viewing Full Images

You can also view the full images and even start them in a slideshow. Select “View Full Images” from the right-most dropdown. Now, when you select a thumbnail, the full image will appear. To start a slideshow, click on “Start Slideshow” on the very bottom left of the image.
