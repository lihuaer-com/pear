Save your favorite images

We’re proud to introduce a brand new feature that allows you to keep track of all of your favorite images and albums.

We noticed that a lot of users have been treating the “liked images” section of their gallery profiles as a favorites list, which means that they were reserving their upvotes for only the very best images that they wanted to track. Or, if the image wasn’t in the gallery, people would often re-upload it into their own account. This was a poor user experience. We want you to be able to feel like you can freely give an upvote to any image you think needs more attention, and we certainly don’t want you to clutter up your own personal account images with images had to upload again.

Keeping these problems in mind, we’re finally ready to release the favorites feature. You can fav any image or album on the site (the hotkey is ’0′), and you can even fav images that are directly linked (where the URL is like http://i.imgur.com/1234567.jpg). Just add /fav to the end of the url (like this: http://i.imgur.com/OJqW4Tr.jpg/fav).

All of your favorite images are stored in a separate section of your account, which you can access from your account menu. This section is completely private. The favorited images here will appear in their simple forms with no gallery comments when you browse them. The images you’ve added to your favorites from the Imgur Gallery will also be shown in the “Gallery favorites” section of your gallery profile and can be browsed by other users similar to the old “Liked images” section.

We’ve also made the decision to get rid of the “Liked images” section in gallery profiles. To do this, we’ve moved all of your liked images into your favorites as a default. If you find that your favorites section is all cluttered up with images that you’ve liked in the past, you can chose to clear out your favorites and start from scratch.

The hotkey to favorite an image is 0.
