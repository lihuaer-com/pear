DPLA and Imgur Present: The Summer of Archives

June 2nd, 2014 by Sarah

Good news, everyone!

We’re ridiculously excited to announce that Imgur is officially teaming up with the Digital Public Library of America (DPLA) to launch an experiment in our gallery from now until August. We’re calling it the Summer of Archives.

DPLA is an all-digital library that aggregates metadata and thumbnails for millions of photographs, manuscripts, books, sounds, moving images, and more from libraries, archives, and museums around the United States. Contributing institutions include the New York Public Library, the Smithsonian, the National Archives, and the Harvard Libraries. They’ve got millions of items from these places instantly searchable and freely available on their site.

The idea of the Summer of Archives is simple: Historical stuff makes the front page all the time. It’s awesome.

As part of the Summer of Archives, DPLA will be digging deep into its archives to post OC gems from its massive collection of content into the gallery. In addition to these posts, there’ll also be chances for the Imgurian community to get involved, and to Q&A with historians and other folks participating with us.

The very first installment — collecting 10 GIFs from historical WW2 footage — is in the gallery right now: http://imgur.com/gallery/rKbep

We’ll be announcing on Twitter and Facebook when these posts go live, so stay tuned.
