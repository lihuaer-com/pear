A few new features this week

Tuesday, November 23rd, 2010

We’ve been trying to get rid of small paper cuts throughout Imgur in order to make for a better user experience. Here’s a list of some cool new stuff this week:

Auto-rotation

Images from cameras that contain EXIF data are now auto-rotated to their correct orientation during the upload.

Better Album Image Sorting

Organizing your albums just got a little easier. It’s now possible to sort your album images by the original file name or by the uploaded date and time of the images.

Add Multiple Images to an Album

It’s always been possible to create an album of multiple images anonymously from the homepage, but now it’s possible to add images to an album after obtaining their links.

If anyone has any suggestions for the next thing to improve, don’t hesitate to tell us! Feel free to contact Alan or leave a post in the forums.

Uploading large images is now 100x faster

Wednesday, November 10th, 2010

Photos taken directly off of cameras can tend to be quite large, typically over 4MB each depending on the settings used. If you wanted to share these large photos, you would have to resize them or wait 5+ minutes for them to upload. Luckily, that’s a thing of the past.

On any modern computer with a high speed connection, uploading images up to 10MB will now only take seconds (probably between 10 and 30) thanks to new client-side processing. Rather than upload the whole 10MB and then resizing it down to something reasonable, Imgur will do the resizing first, and then upload the image now that it’s much smaller. This drastically cuts down on the time that it takes to upload. However, this is only possible while using the Flash uploader (which is the default is most cases): http://imgur.com/?flash. Of course, if you’re not a fan of Adobe Flash, then you can always use the old-school HTML uploader here: http://imgur.com/?noFlash.

Note that this is not additional processing that’s done on the image – it’s the same that it’s always been – it’s just faster!

Faster uploading is a feature that everyone can enjoy. However, we understand that sometimes you want to keep your images in the highest quality possible, regardless of how long it takes you to upload them. That’s why Pro users (upgrade here!) can easily toggle this new feature on and off. Just visit your settings and check “Upload images in high quality.”

The Hottest Images Online

Monday, November 1st, 2010

The new Gallery.

It’s always been a bit of a challenge for us to determine which images make it into the Imgur Gallery and which ones don’t. The Gallery is a place for funny and interesting pictures, but until now, every image was hand-picked to be in it based on a set of criteria. This was good because the images were guaranteed to be good ones, but even worse because updates were painfully slow.

The new Gallery is leaps and bounds better than the old, and will get you hooked at viewing all of the thousands of awesome images in it. Better yet, you can even submit your own captions for the images, and vote them up and down if you like or dislike them. Images that are voted up are shown at the top of the list, and images that are voted down are shown at the bottom, or may disappear all together. There may even be prizes for people who upload the highest voted image of the day. It’s also possible to vote on the captions of the image. The highest voted caption is displayed prominently under the image, and the worst captions are displayed at the very bottom.

How it works.

To get your image into the Gallery, it must be spread all over the Internet and gain a certain level of popularity. This means that you’re still able to upload images and have them remain private (just don’t share them all over the Internet!) while still being able to view the best images out there.

The “best” images are determined by you guys and the Internet community as a whole. However, we’d like to specifically thank the Twitter search engine Topsy. Topsy makes it very easy to search Twitter for the top trending Imgur images. We then take what we learn from Topsy, and look at our own data to determine if something is popular. However, nothing is perfect, so if you find an image you don’t like, just vote it down!

If you want your image to be spotlighted in the Gallery, then start sharing it on the Internet. Submit it to Reddit, tweet it on Twitter, talk about it on your blog, etc. Oh, and it would help if it was a picture of a cat.
